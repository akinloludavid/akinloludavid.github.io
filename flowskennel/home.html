7 UNKNOWN BENEFITS OF HAVING A DOG

  1. Dogs are brilliant companions:There is never a dull moment around dogs. It is almost impossible to feel lonely when you have dogs around you. Dogs are very sociable animals and they love being around humans. So whenever you feel bored, grab a toy and spend some time with your pup.

  2. Dogs can save your life:Owing to the fact that dogs are very sensitive and intelligent animals, they can detect cancer and other diseases in the body of their owners. There have been stories of pups who continued to lick and sniff at moles or lumps on their human’s bodies, who eventually found out that those skin conditions were cancerous. In fact some dogs are trained to detect cancer within the space of 3 hours.

  3. Humans with dogs have a high rate of recovering from sickness: People recover faster from illnesses and even have higher survival rates after a heart attack if they have a dog. THis is actually a scientifically proven facts.

  4. They protect their little mates: Children who grow up with dogs have a lower risk of developing eczema than those who have cats or no dogs at all. Also it builds a sense of responsibility in children when they feed and take care of dogs.

  5. Having a dog increases your physical well-being and encourages healthy lifestyle: Dog owners carry a responsibility of training and playing with their dogs. This makes dog owners to be more physically active.

  6. They are a reflection of your personality: Even though dogs are naturally jovial and playful but if you are the kind that flares up frequently, you might end up having an aggressive dog.

  7. They are perfect for security and protection: This is a fact almost everyone knows. There are special breeds that are meant for protection and security like the German-sherperd, rottweiler. However even the less fierce dogs are great at alerting humans when they sense intrusion or danger.

  2. Dogs are intelligent enough to differentiate between new things and old things:

  3. You yawn around your dogs, they yawn too:
